Because our school in Pefine De Areia is located in an isolated area, we have an incredibly high illiteracy rate. Most of the people live so far away from our school that their only chance of obtaining an education is to walk miles to attend a couple of hours in this school. We have many kids who have to start school late due to the distance. The village of Pefine De Areia is a very poor village and almost every person who lives here, including the parents of all these kids, are unemployed.

They fight every day simply to survive and feed their families. Some of them try to fish, some carry sand from the beach and try to sell it, and some work contraction and try to re-build homes for each other. In conclusion, 90% of all our student's parents do not have jobs, yet they live every day doing whatever they can to make the smallest amount of money. The women work in harvest time to produce chest nuts that they sell for money.

This school has been a true blessing for these kids. Now they have the unique opportuni ty to attend a school where they can learn how to speak the official language of their country: Portuguese. Here they also learn principles of the bible and receive daily meals which makes an incredible difference in their lives: we are substituting starvation with education. We thank God which is the true source of this project and every day we are in awe of what he has done. It can definitely be said that our mission is a lot work, but we have a God who rises up his people to help his work.

Many church's and organizations are contributing to this project, so hopefully it may never stop expanding and just grow bigger! We thank all of you who made the decision to be a part of this project which is been incredibly important to our community. With your help we can build more classrooms in our school and possibly build another school! We would like to especially thank Pr. Nilson and Dr. Xu for your tremendous efforts and support!

Vanessa Fraga
School Director
